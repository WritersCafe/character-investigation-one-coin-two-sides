H3

Trace the Decision into an Event

What happens because Brock makes that decision?
⌄
🧠 Thinking Coach: A character drives the story when a decision causes, prevents, changes, or redirects an event.
Ask:
  • What event happens next?
  • What would not have happened without this choice?
  • Does the choice create a new problem?
Look for plot movement:
  • a conflict grows
  • a relationship changes
  • a new event begins
  • the story turns in a new direction
Because Brock decides to ____, ____ happens.
This decision changes the direction of the story by ____.
Without Brock’s decision to ____, the event ____ would not happen.

TAILS — HOW IS THE STORY SHAPING THE CHARACTER?
T1

Choose an Incident Report

The event is provided. Your job is to analyze how Brock responds and how that response shapes him.
⌄
🧠 Thinking Coach: STAAR-style questions often point you to an important moment. Choose one incident, then focus your thinking on the relationship: event → response → change.
Choose one incident before moving on. Record its letter and event in your notebook.
T2

Name the Challenge or Pressure

What does this event require Brock to face, understand, or decide?
⌄
🧠 Thinking Coach: Events shape characters by creating pressure. Explain what is difficult, new, risky, or emotionally important about your selected incident.
This event challenges Brock to ____.
The event creates pressure because Brock must ____.
This moment confronts Brock with ____.
T3

Study Brock’s Response

What does Brock think, feel, say, decide, or do because of the incident?
⌄
🧠 Thinking Coach: The event does not shape Brock automatically. His response is the bridge between what happens and how he changes.
Look for a response:
  • something Brock says
  • a decision he makes
  • an action he takes
  • a feeling he accepts
  • a belief he begins to question
Use your organizer when helpful:
  • Which recorded detail supports the response?
  • What did you say that detail reveals?
Brock responds to this incident by ____.
Instead of ____, Brock ____.
His words / actions / thoughts show that ____.
T4

Explain How the Response Shapes Brock

How is he being challenged, changed, strengthened, or redirected?
⌄
🧠 Thinking Coach: Do not stop with “Brock changes.” Name the direction of the change. Explain what he now feels, values, understands, or is willing to do.
Because Brock responds by ____, he becomes more / less ____.
This experience shapes Brock by teaching him ____.
Brock begins to value / understand / question ____.
This incident moves Brock from ____ toward ____.
